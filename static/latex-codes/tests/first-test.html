Theorem 1.1 (Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic). Every integer greater than 1 either is a prime itself or can be represented as the product of primes, unique up to order.

Lemma 1.2 (Euclid’s Lemma). If a prime \( p \) divides the product \( ab \), then \( p \) divides \( a \) or \( p \) divides \( b \).

Corollary 1.3. There are inﬁnitely many primes.
